🎯What is Poker Trainer?

Poker Trainer is a decision-making tool designed to sharpen your Texas Hold'em skills. Instead of playing full hands, you are dropped into a specific scenario mid-hand and asked to make the best possible decision.

Each scenario has a correct answer based on poker theory and equity calculations. After submitting, you'll see whether your decision was optimal. Over time, your correct decision percentage will reflect genuine skill improvement.

📋
Scenario Based
You are placed mid-hand and must decide the best action given the board and opponents.
📈
Track Progress
Your win rate and XP are tracked so you can watch yourself improve session by session.
🏆
Compete
Your stats appear on the leaderboard so you can compare decision-making against other players.
🃏Texas Hold'em Basics

Each player is dealt 2 private hole cards. Over the course of the hand, 5 community cards are revealed. The goal is the best possible 5-card hand using any combination of hole cards and community cards.

Pre-Flop
2 hole cards dealt. Blinds posted. First betting round.
Flop
3 community cards revealed. Second betting round.
Turn
4th community card. Third betting round.
River
5th card revealed. Final betting round before showdown.

⚡Actions

On your turn you have several options depending on the current state of the hand.

Check
Pass with no bet when no bet has been made.
Fold
Discard your hand and forfeit chips already in the pot.
Call
Match the current bet to stay in the hand.
Bet
Place a wager when no bet has been made. Sizes shown as % of pot.
Raise
Increase an existing bet, forcing others to respond.
All In
Bet your entire remaining stack. Maximum aggression.
💰Big Blinds (BB)

This app measures stacks and pots in Big Blinds (BB) — the professional standard. It removes stake size from the equation, so a decision correct at $1/$2 is equally correct at $25/$50 when measured in BB.

📐
Example: If the big blind is $2 and you have $200, you have 100 BB. All players start with 100 BB, representing a standard deep-stack cash game scenario.
🧠Opponent Playstyles

Each opponent has an assigned playstyle reflecting their tendencies. Understanding opponents is core to correct decisions.

🪨
NIT
Extremely tight. Only premium hands. If they're in the pot, be careful.
🎯
TAG
Tight-Aggressive. Selective but plays aggressively. Most common winning style.
🔥
LAG
Loose-Aggressive. Plays many hands aggressively. Unpredictable and hard to read.
😴
Passive
Loose-Passive. Plays many hands but rarely bets. A classic "calling station".
